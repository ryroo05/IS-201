Cambodia

My 18-Month Experience

Serving a Mission in Cambodia

I spent 18 months in the Cambodia Phnom Penh West Mission, teaching, serving, and learning to speak the Khmer language. This page shares a few things I loved about the culture, holidays, and people.

Cambodian Culture Highlights

Cambodia is warm, loud, and full of scooters and smiles. The official language is Khmer, written in a beautiful script. One simple greeting is សួស្តី (suos-sih-dei) which means “hello.”

Daily life is centered around family, food, and the local wat (Buddhist temple). Street markets open early, and you can buy anything from fresh mangoes to live chickens.

Holidays I Experienced

Here are a few of the big holidays I remember, plus what they’re like on the ground:

  1. Khmer New Year (Chaul Chnam Thmey)
    • Lasts three days in April and feels like the entire country is on vacation.
    • Families travel back to their hometowns to visit parents and grandparents.
    • Lots of games, music, and sometimes huge water fights in the streets.
  2. Pchum Ben (Ancestors’ Day)
    • A 15-day religious festival focused on honoring ancestors.
    • People bring offerings to temples very early in the morning.
    • We were often invited to eat traditional foods with local families.
  3. Water Festival (Bon Om Touk)
    • Celebrates the reversing flow of the Tonlé Sap River.
    • Huge boat races in Phnom Penh, with crews from all over the country.
    • Vendors line the river selling snacks, drinks, and glowing toys at night.

Khmer Phrase of the Day

Try saying this simple phrase:

អរគុណ (aw-kun) – “thank you”

Depending on who you’re talking to, you often add a polite ending like lokpu (for men) or nakming (for women).
